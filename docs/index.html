Methodology

Comparable enough to use.
Different enough to explain.

States set their own tests and performance labels. This project preserves those original labels, then identifies the closest available indicator of students performing at the lowest level.

01

Exact state tiers

We use the share in the state’s lowest published performance tier, such as Level 1, Novice, or Does Not Meet Expectations.

02

Other state sources

Where the state publishes only a proficiency rate or a combined non-proficient bin, we retain it with a distinct label rather than presenting it as an exact tier.

03

Federal fallback

Ed Data Express fills remaining gaps as 100 minus percent proficient. These older values provide coverage, but they are not a strict below-basic measure.

!

Do not rank without context

Assessment standards, years, and reporting structures vary. Use this dashboard to compare coverage and reported outcomes, not as a precise league table.

NAEP methodology

Comparable by design.
Careful by necessity.

NAEP assesses a representative sample using a common national framework. These results describe jurisdictions and student groups, not individual students or schools.

01

Common measure

Every jurisdiction shown here uses the 2024 NAEP Grade 4 Reading framework, unlike state assessments built around different state standards.

02

Below Basic

The displayed value is 100 minus the official percentage at or above NAEP Basic. NAEP achievement levels are not equivalent to state proficiency categories.

03

Public-school benchmark

The national comparison is the 2024 public-school result, matching the population represented in the state-level NAEP rows.

!

Not a significance test

Small percentage differences may reflect sampling variation. This view does not claim that every ordered difference is statistically significant.

Source: National Center for Education Statistics, 2024 NAEP Grade 4 Reading.
